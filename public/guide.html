How to Use the App

A simple guide for family & friends

This app puts Mohammad Shafi's complete Qur'aanic Studies — all 114 Surahs with Arabic, transliteration, English translation and his verse-by-verse notes — in your pocket. No account needed to read.

Getting Started Reading a Surah Tafsir Notes References Navigation Search Saving Verses Verse of the Day Tips
01

Getting Started

Opening the app for the first time.

🌐
Open the link
Tap the link shared with you. The app opens directly in your browser — no download or installation required. It works on any phone, tablet or computer.
📖
Tap "Begin Reading"
The landing page shows a Verse of the Day and introduces the app. Tap Begin Reading or Browse Surahs to enter.
🌙
Switch to Dark Mode (optional)
Tap the moon/sun icon in the top-right to switch between light and dark themes. Your preference is saved automatically.
Add to Home Screen: In your phone's browser, tap Share → "Add to Home Screen" to save the app as an icon, just like a real app.
02

Reading a Surah

Every verse has Arabic text, romanised pronunciation, and an English translation.

📋
Choose a Surah
The Surahs screen lists all 114 chapters. You can filter by Meccan or Medinan (when each chapter was revealed), or type a name or number in the search box at the top.
🔤
Each verse shows three layers
Arabic — the original Qur'aanic text
Transliteration — how to pronounce it in English letters
Translation — Mohammad Shafi's English meaning
🔠
Adjust the font size
Tap A− or A+ in the top-right corner to make text smaller or larger. Your setting is remembered next time.
💾
Resume where you left off
The app remembers the last verse you were reading. When you return, tap Continue Reading on the home screen to pick up exactly where you stopped.
2:255 Al-Baqarah · Ayatul Kursi
ٱللَّهُ لَآ إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ ٱلْحَىُّ ٱلْقَيُّومُ
Allahu la ilaha illa huwa al-Hayyu al-Qayyum
Allah — there is no deity except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer of existence.
03

Mohammad Shafi's Tafsir Notes

The heart of the app — verse-by-verse explanations drawn entirely from the Qur'aan itself.

📝
Notes appear below each verse
When a verse has a study note from Mohammad Shafi, it appears in a shaded box below the translation. These explain the deeper meaning, historical context, and connections to other verses.
👁
Show / Hide individual notes
Each verse with a note has a Hide / Note button on the right. Tap it to collapse or expand that note individually.
🔘
Toggle all notes at once
The Notes toggle switch in the top control bar turns all notes on or off across the whole Surah at once — useful if you just want to read the translation without commentary.
Which Surahs have notes? Notes are available across all 114 Surahs — this is the complete text of the Modern Tafsir.
04

Qur'aanic References

Mohammad Shafi frequently references other verses. You can read them with one tap.

🔗
Tap a reference like [2:255]
Inside the notes you'll see references in golden brackets — for example [4:82] or [2:67-71]. Tap any of them and a panel slides up showing that verse instantly, without leaving your current place.
📑
Verse ranges show all verses
A range like [18:65-82] means verses 65 through 82 of Surah 18. Tapping it shows all the verses in that range, stacked in the same panel.
✕
Close the panel
Tap the ✕ button or tap anywhere outside the panel to close it and return to reading.
05

Jumping to Any Surah or Verse

You don't have to scroll — jump directly to where you want to go.

📂
Surah dropdown
Inside the reader, there is a dropdown in the control bar listing all 114 Surahs by name. Select any Surah and it navigates there instantly — no button needed.
🔢
Jump to a verse
Type a verse number in the verse box next to the dropdown (e.g. 255) and the page scrolls to that verse automatically after a moment, or press Enter immediately.
⬆
Scroll back to top
A green arrow button appears at the bottom-right once you scroll down. Tap it to return to the top of the Surah instantly.
⏭
Next / Previous Surah
At the bottom of every Surah are two buttons to go to the previous or next Surah in order — useful for reading consecutively.
06

Searching the Qur'aan

Find any word or phrase across all 114 Surahs in seconds.

🔍
Tap the Search tab
The Search tab is in the bottom navigation bar. Type any English word — from the translation, transliteration, or the tafsir notes.
🏷
Filter what you search
Use the filter pills — All, Translation, or Notes — to narrow results. For example, select Notes to search only within the tafsir commentary.
↗
Tap a result to open it
Each result card shows the verse reference, Arabic, and a snippet of matching text. Tap any card to open that verse in the reader.
Tip: Try searching "mercy", "patience", or "prayer" to explore what the Qur'aan and the tafsir say about a topic.
07

Saving Verses

Bookmark any verse to revisit it later.

🔖
Tap "Save" on any verse
Each verse card has a Save button. Tap it to bookmark that verse. It turns to ✓ Saved to confirm. Tap again to remove it.
📚
View your saved verses
Tap the Saved tab in the bottom bar. Your bookmarks are shown with options to sort by All, By Surah, or Recent.
☁
Sign in to sync across devices (optional)
Sign in with your Google account from the Saved screen to sync bookmarks across all your devices — phone, tablet, and computer.
08

Verse of the Day

A different verse every day, chosen from across the Qur'aan.

🌅
Shown on the home screen
Every day a new verse is featured on the landing page with its Arabic text and English translation. The selection cycles through 536 meaningful verses — including the cross-references from Mohammad Shafi's own notes — so you will rarely see the same verse twice.
📖
Tap "Read with Tafsir"
Tap the Read with Tafsir → button on the verse card to open that exact verse in the reader — including Mohammad Shafi's note if one exists for it.
09

Quick Tips

A few things worth knowing.

📵
Works offline (partially)
Once a Surah has been opened, it stays cached in your browser and can be read without internet.
📋
Copy any verse
Each verse has a Copy button. It copies the Arabic, transliteration and translation together — ready to paste into WhatsApp or Notes.
🔢
Verse references
References like 2:255 mean Surah 2, Verse 255. The first number is always the Surah, the second is the verse.
🕌
Meccan vs Medinan
The Surah list filter lets you view only Meccan surahs (revealed before the migration) or Medinan ones (after). 86 are Meccan, 28 are Medinan.
📰
Blog
The Blog tab contains posts by Mohammad Shafi. Posts can be shared directly to WhatsApp or Email from inside the post.
📚
PDF Books
The Books section lets you download or read the original Qur'aanic Studies volumes as PDF files.
